1. Executive Summary
Product teams at SaaS companies, e-commerce platforms, and mobile apps collectively drown in thousands of unstructured feedback signals monthly — app store reviews, support tickets, social mentions, and interview notes. Without systematic analysis, high-signal insights go unnoticed while engineering prioritizes based on loudest voices rather than actual user impact.

The Customer Feedback Analyzer and Prioritization Agent (CFAPA) solves this by deploying an AI-powered pipeline — built on the Antigravity platform and Codex AI framework — that ingests feedback from all sources, extracts themes and sentiment automatically, and surfaces prioritized, evidence-backed product recommendations directly into teams' existing PM workflows.

CFAPA is designed for teams receiving 1,000–500,000 feedback items per month who want to replace ad-hoc analysis with a systematic, data-driven feedback intelligence layer.

2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Core Problem
Product teams lack a systematic, scalable method to analyze qualitative feedback. Current pain points include:
Manual analysis is slow: reviewing 5,000 tickets monthly takes ~80 analyst hours
Coverage is incomplete: teams typically sample <10% of feedback due to volume
Insight quality is biased: loudest customer voices, not highest-impact themes, drive roadmaps
No quantification: qualitative feedback cannot be compared against revenue or segment impact
Fragmentation: insights live in Slack, spreadsheets, and individual team members' heads

4. Target Users and Personas
4.1 Primary Persona — The Overwhelmed PM
Alex, 34 — Senior Product Manager at a B2B SaaS company (Series B, 200 employees)
Alex manages a product used by 8,000 business customers. Each month, 12,000 support tickets and 3,000 app reviews arrive. Alex currently samples ~200 items per month and maintains a shared spreadsheet to track themes. Critical feature requests are missed. Sprint planning feels like guesswork.
CFAPA gives Alex an always-on intelligence layer that highlights the top 10 themes every week and auto-creates Linear tickets for the top-ranked ones — saving 15+ hours/month and ensuring no critical signal is missed.

4.2 Secondary Persona — The Customer Success Lead
Priya, 29 — Customer Success Manager at a mobile-first e-commerce platform
Priya handles 40 enterprise accounts and needs early warnings when sentiment deteriorates before it leads to churn. She currently relies on reactive support escalations. CFAPA's alerting feature lets Priya see a sentiment score drop for a specific customer segment before they cancel.

4.3 Tertiary Persona — The Data-Driven CPO
Marcus, 42 — Chief Product Officer at a SaaS company (growth stage, 500+ employees)
Marcus needs board-level metrics on product-market fit derived from customer feedback trends. He wants a quarterly digest that shows which themes are growing, which were addressed, and how feedback sentiment correlates with revenue cohorts.

5.2 Core Feature Descriptions
5.2.1 Multi-Source Feedback Ingestion
Connect and ingest feedback from app stores, support platforms (Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk), social media (Twitter/X, Reddit), user interview transcripts, and CSV uploads. Support both scheduled polling and real-time webhook modes. Deduplicate using content fingerprinting.

5.2.2 Sentiment Analysis
Per-item sentiment classification (5-class) and aspect-level sentiment by product area. Powered by a Codex fine-tuned model. Accuracy target: 85% precision, 82% recall. Sentiment scores feed into ICS computation and alerting.

5.2.3 Topic Modeling and Theme Clustering
Unsupervised clustering of feedback into recurring themes using BERTopic via Codex. Auto-generated human-readable labels per theme. Weekly or on-demand refresh. Admin tools to merge, rename, and archive themes. Each theme shows trend direction, item count, and sentiment distribution.

5.2.4 Impact Confidence Score (ICS)
A composite prioritization score combining request frequency (30%), revenue-weighted user segment (30%), sentiment severity (20%), and recency trend (20%). Configurable weights. Produces a ranked priority queue refreshed per ingestion cycle. Top 10% flagged for immediate PM review.

5.2.5 PM Tool Integration
One-click issue creation in Jira (REST), Linear (GraphQL), and Productboard (REST). Issues pre-populated with recommendation summary, evidence quotes, ICS score, and affected user count. Bi-directional sync: 'Done' in PM tool marks theme as 'Addressed' in CFAPA.

5.2.6 LLM-Powered Recommendations
For each top-ranked theme, CFAPA generates a structured recommendation: executive summary, 3-5 evidence quotes, affected segments, suggested action type, and estimated effort tier. Generated via Codex LLM with a structured, versioned prompt template ensuring consistent format and traceable citations.

7. UX and Design Requirements
7.1 Design Principles
Clarity over complexity: insights should be scannable in under 60 seconds
Evidence-first: every recommendation must link to supporting feedback items
Embedded in workflow: minimize context-switching via deep PM tool integration
Progressive disclosure: summary views with drill-down for analysts who want depth
